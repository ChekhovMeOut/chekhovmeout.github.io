Summary

Background

GenerationBio (GenBio) is a rising biotechnology startup in the Boston area. It is pioneering a new class of genetic medicine, lipid nanoparticles (LNPs), which differ substantially from traditional AAV therapies. Lipid nanoparticles can carry much longer payloads, are re-dosable, easily customized, and elicit a more consistent inflammatory response. GenBio seeks to solve the remaining challenges, like sinusoidal entrapment and species translation, to usher in a new era of genetic medicine.

My Role

My responsibility on the drug delivery team was to help achieve the proper biodistribution and stability of the final drug product. Specifically, I worked in the analytical assay group (AAG) which developed many of the techniques used to assess particle characteristics.

I developed three unique assays at GenBio, but I learned to execute and understand many more:

1. The pKa assay is a theoretically well-established method. I conducted process optimization digitally through documentation and analysis, and physically using new automation equipment.

2. The endosomal stability assay is a novel approach I developed for assessing LNP stability. LNPs are exposed to in vivo conditions while the rate and mechanism of their decay is recorded. This approach proved highly successful at predicting species translation - often a difficult and expensive hurdle in biotechnology. In comparison to traditional non-human primate autopsies it offers a dramatic cost saving of almost 1000x.

3. The endosomal escape assay was by far my most challenging project. It was intended to study the fusing kinetics between LNPs and cell surfaces. I was unable to complete its development during my co-op period, but I made valuable contributions for its future use.

My routine responsibilities included making preclinical drug product formulations for in vivo testing in animals. This required careful chemical handling, batch control, and sterile technique. When not creating drug product or developing assays, I executed assays for other teams and quickly returned the analyzed data using Prism & Benchling. I gained additional experience with technical communication, culminating in a company-wide presentation of my work.

Culture

GenerationBio boasted one of the best working cultures I have experienced yet. Individual contributions were recognized instantly. Communication between leadership and employees was frequent. I developed excellent working relationships with my mentors, made rapid progress, and consistently stayed busy.

Progressive, forward-thinking policies dramatically improved the work environment and team cohesion. The DEI efforts made at GenBio were sincere and effective. I found myself loving the results-oriented startup and stayed on full time after my official co-op period ended.
