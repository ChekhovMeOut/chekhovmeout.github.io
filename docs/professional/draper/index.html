Summary

Background

Draper is a historic company which began as a MIT research group in 1933. It has since evolved into the preeminent defense contractor for the most sensitive US guidance and control programs. Draper is perhaps best known for designing the Apollo missions' guidance systems, including the first human landing on the moon during Apollo 11. They have continued to build their historical legacy with modern defense projects including the Trident missile program and Cygnus ISS resupplying program.

My Role

My position as a chemist and materials scientist was focused on engineering and development of large, existing projects. This experience helped me understand the nuances between the breakneck, results-driven pace of research compared to the methodical, careful nature of engineering. I am unable to share photographs or program specifics, but I can discuss my work in the abstract.

I supported aerospace systems by validating specialized components with a battery of thermomechanical tests. I gained fundamental understanding of the theory underpinning disc scanning calorimetry (DSC), thermogravimetric analysis (TGA), dynamic mechanical analysis (DMA), and others. Similarly, I gained experience with specialized and sensitive pieces of equipment such as scanning electron microscopy (SEM), energy dispersive x-ray (EDX), fourier transfer infrared spectroscopy (FTIR) and raman spectroscopy.

I conducted two distinct research projects in addition to daily engineering support. One involved the development of novel electrochemical plating techniques, which leveraged previous knowledge from previous research. The second project required VBA programming to algorithmically optimize aerospace power system design specifications.

Culture

Draper is brimming with highly educated and experienced professionals. This wealth of knowledge and and physical resources meant that virtually nothing was out of reach for study. However, this was tempered by the effects of export controls, project classification, and federal reporting requirements. The highly established structure at Draper showed me the virtues and challenges of such massive projects.

The siloed nature of work at Draper demanded frequent independent thinking and problem solving. Meticulous time management and reporting skills were essential for meeting the company's stringent policies. I enjoyed my work at Draper and stayed on part-time after my co-op officially ended.
