Summary

Background

Blue Origin is a private space company founded in 2000 with the goal of making spaceflight accessible to everyone. As a unique aerospace company with sites all across the United States, it seeks to radically reduce the cost of access to space with bold, manufacturing-first operational approaches. At its Huntsville, AL location, Blue Origin essentially a blank canvas; it has since transformed to regularly launching its suborbital New Shepard rocket and more recently, the heavy-lift New Glenn rocket.

My Role

My responsibilities were to innovate, streamline, and support chemical processing operations at the engine manufacturing facility. Specifically, I specialized in cleaning, passivation, and verification (CPV) operations. My daily tasks included analyzing salt and process delays, reviewing test results, and making recommendations intended to focus mostly on the momentous BE-4 engine which generates over half a million pounds of thrust. The engine was also under the most scheduling pressure, which gave me real-time opportunities to see the effects of quality control on an extremely precise process.

One of my major projects was to use statistical process control (SPC) to improve throughput and decrease variations in the process. First, I improved workflows over a documentation to reduce redundancy, decrease variability, and improve batch traceability. I then developed a proof-of-concept to continually assess performance based on historical CPV operations from these workflows. The enabled rigorous analysis of our process stability and capability where I could identify critical gaps in our process control for different allowable criteria.

Another major responsibility of this role was to perform audits on both internal and external systems, including calibration maintenance projects and inventory chain issues involving the development of ideal acid (HCl) operating capabilities. Externally, I performed supplier audits on Blue Origin's "special processes," some of which regularly deal with Hydrazine and Ammonia.
