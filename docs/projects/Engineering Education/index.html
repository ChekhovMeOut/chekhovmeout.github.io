Summary

Background

In 2023, I joined the Science The World. research group run by Professor Landherr. We seek to address institutional disparities in higher education, which are especially prevalent in disciplines like chemical engineering, by developing novel and accessible educational materials. This includes low-cost experimental demonstrations, interactive virtual simulations, and informative comics.

Objective

Professor Landherr recently won a $300,000 NSF grant to create open-access educational materials and study their implementation at universities across the U.S. The grant is specifically awarded to produce art-drive STEM comics on 16 subjects in chemical engineering. The full list (see below) features complex topics including thermodynamics, phase analysis, and material balances.

My Role

I wrote the script for the atom balance comic. I found it to be a challenging yet rewarding topic; it needs strong conceptual explanations to differentiate it from other topics (I.e., mass and mol) and mathematical worked examples. Combining these requirements into a limited space with an uncontrived and engaging story line was difficult. Synthesizing complex topics in the role of a teacher, rather than a student, was also a novel and stimulating experience.

My script is now in the final stages of editing. It will soon be turned over to the artist to be visually rendered. I will post the final product here when it is available.
